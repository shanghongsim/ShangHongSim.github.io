I rebuilt this site, gave it a proper domain, and decided it was finally time to start writing.

I work on post-training at [Oumi](https://oumi.ai), and my research time goes into making LLMs reliable: grounding, citation quality, and knowing when a model should refuse. A lot of what I learn day to day never makes it into papers. Training recipes that worked, the many that didn't, evaluation gotchas, and the occasional detour into biology, which is where I started before tech.

That's what this blog is for. Short, honest notes rather than polished essays.

If something here helps you, or you think I got something wrong, [email me](mailto:simshanghong@gmail.com). I'd genuinely like to hear it.
